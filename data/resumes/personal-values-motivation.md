# Personal Values & Career Motivation

## Core Values

### 1. Continuous Learning

- **In Trades**: Always learning new techniques, materials, and safety standards
- **In Tech**: Staying current with rapidly evolving technologies and best practices
- **Future Goal**: Never stop learning, whether it's a new framework or a new domain

### 2. Problem-Solving

- **Why It Matters**: I get energized by tackling complex challenges and finding elegant solutions
- **Trades Example**: Diagnosing why an electrical system failed and fixing it permanently
- **Tech Example**: Debugging a complex issue and implementing a robust solution

### 3. Making an Impact

- **Trades Impact**: Making buildings safer and more energy efficient
- **Tech Impact**: Building software that helps people work better and live easier
- **Long-term Goal**: Build technology that bridges the gap between physical and digital worlds

### 4. Quality Craftsmanship

- **Trades**: Taking pride in work that lasts and performs well
- **Tech**: Writing clean, maintainable code that other developers can build upon
- **Philosophy**: Whether it's insulation or code, do it right the first time

## Career Motivation Statement

"I transitioned from trades to technology because I wanted to solve problems at scale. While I enjoyed the immediate satisfaction of fixing physical systems, I was drawn to how software could solve problems for thousands of people simultaneously. My background in trades gave me a unique perspective - I understand both the physical and digital worlds, which allows me to see opportunities for technology that others might miss."

## What Drives Me

### Technical Curiosity

- I love understanding how things work, whether it's an electrical panel or a distributed system
- I'm naturally drawn to complex systems that require deep understanding
- I enjoy the process of breaking down complex problems into manageable parts

### Helping Others

- In trades, I helped create safer, more efficient buildings
- In tech, I want to build tools that help people work better
- I enjoy mentoring and sharing knowledge with others

### Building vs Maintaining

- While I'm good at maintenance and troubleshooting, my real passion is building new things
- I prefer creating solutions from scratch rather than just fixing existing problems
- This is why I love full-stack development - I can build entire systems

## Long-term Vision

I want to be the kind of developer who:

- Understands both the technical and business implications of decisions
- Can bridge communication between technical and non-technical teams
- Builds software that solves real-world problems, not just technical challenges
- Continues growing and learning throughout my career

My unique background in trades gives me a perspective that most developers don't have - I understand how things work in the real world, not just on screen. This makes me better at building practical, usable solutions.
