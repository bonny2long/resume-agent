# Career Transition Story: From Trades to Tech

## Why I Made the Transition

After 6+ years working as an Electrical Technician and Heat & Frost Insulator, I discovered that my true passion was problem-solving through technology. While I valued the hands-on technical work in trades, I found myself increasingly drawn to how software could solve complex, systematic problems that physical labor alone couldn't address.

## The Turning Point

During my time as an Electrical Technician at Alexander Dennis Inc., I became fascinated with the diagnostic systems and software that controlled complex electrical systems. I realized that the troubleshooting skills I had developed - understanding complex systems, methodical problem-solving, and attention to detail - were exactly what made great software engineers.

## Transferable Skills from Trades

### Problem-Solving Mindset

- **Trades**: Diagnosing complex electrical systems and insulation challenges
- **Tech**: Debugging code and architecting software solutions

### Technical Precision

- **Trades**: Reading complex schematics and blueprints
- **Tech**: Understanding system architecture and API documentation

### Project Management

- **Trades**: Managing multi-million dollar insulation projects with tight deadlines
- **Tech**: Leading software development projects from concept to deployment

### Team Collaboration

- **Trades**: Working with diverse teams (engineers, project managers, clients)
- **Tech**: Collaborating in cross-functional development teams

### Safety and Quality Focus

- **Trades**: Ensuring safety protocols and quality standards
- **Tech**: Implementing secure coding practices and thorough testing

## The Decision to Pivot

I chose i.c Stars specifically because it offered a comprehensive technology education combined with business and leadership development. This aligned perfectly with my goal of not just becoming a developer, but a well-rounded technology professional who understands both the technical and business aspects of software solutions.

## Vision for the Future

My background gives me a unique perspective - I understand both physical and digital systems, which allows me to see opportunities for technology solutions that others might miss. I want to build software that solves real-world problems, especially in industries that have been slow to adopt technology.

This transition isn't just about changing careers - it's about combining the best of both worlds: the practical, hands-on problem-solving from trades with the infinite possibilities of technology.
